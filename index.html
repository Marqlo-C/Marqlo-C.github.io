About
Engineering things that help the folks around me.

I spent most of my early career in enterprise tech sales and before that the Navy, getting decently skilled at understanding what kinds of tools people required to more easily complete complex tasks while lacking any ability to deliver on most of it. That technical gap bugged me enough that I eventually decided to go back to school. Now I’m a senior at UC Davis studying CompSci.

I’ve always been the type that prefers to see how all the pieces fit together or I’ll lose sleep over it, which is probably also why I dissect films a little too much in my free time. Shot compositions, pacing, scripts, all of it. A few people around me may have expressed that that aspect of me might be bordering on esoteric... but hey, what can you do.

Anyways, I came to this later than most but when something is genuinely new for everyone I tend to figure it out pretty quick. I care about building things that actually solve problems for people, the kind of stuff that makes someone’s day a little easier. That’s enough for me to take it seriously.
